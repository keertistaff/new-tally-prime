Introduction

Financial Statements are the final reports generated from accounting records that show the financial position and performance of a business.

After recording all transactions in TallyPrime using vouchers, the software automatically prepares various financial statements.

These reports help:

  • Business Owners
  • Accountants
  • Managers
  • Investors
  • Banks
  • Government Authorities

understand how the business is performing financially.

In TallyPrime, financial statements are generated automatically based on ledger balances and voucher entries.

What are Financial Statements?

Financial Statements are reports that summarize:

  1. Income earned
  2. Expenses incurred
  3. Assets owned
  4. Liabilities owed
  5. Profit earned or loss incurred

They provide a complete picture of the financial health of a business.

Major Financial Statements in TallyPrime

1. Balance Sheet

Shows:

  • Assets
  • Liabilities
  • Capital

at a specific date.

2. Profit & Loss Account

Shows:

  • Income
  • Expenses
  • Net Profit or Loss

for a specific period.

3. Cash Flow Statement

Shows:

  • Cash Inflows
  • Cash Outflows

during a period.

4. Fund Flow Statement

Shows movement of funds within the business.

5. Ratio Analysis

Shows financial ratios used for business decision making.

6. Trial Balance

Shows all ledger balances before final accounts are prepared.

Real-Life Example

Suppose Rohit starts a mobile shop.

Transactions

  • Invested ₹5,00,000
  • Purchased mobiles ₹3,00,000
  • Sold mobiles ₹4,50,000
  • Paid rent ₹20,000
  • Paid salary ₹30,000

At month-end, Rohit wants to know:

  • How much profit he earned

  • How much he owes suppliers

These answers come from Financial Statements.

1. Balance Sheet

Meaning

Balance Sheet shows the financial position of a business on a particular date.

It follows:

Assets = Liabilities + Capital

Structure of Balance Sheet

  • Capital Account
  • Loans
  • Creditors
  • Outstanding Expenses

Assets Side

  • Cash
  • Bank Balance
  • Stock
  • Furniture
  • Building
  • Debtors

2. Profit & Loss Account

Meaning

Profit & Loss Account shows:

  • Business Income
  • Business Expenses
  • Net Profit or Net Loss

during a specific period.

Formula

Profit = Income − Expenses

If expenses exceed income:

Loss = Expenses − Income

Example

Income

Particulars Amount
Sales ₹5,00,000

Expenses

Particulars Amount
Rent ₹20,000
Salary ₹40,000
Electricity ₹10,000
Total ₹70,000

Profit:

₹5,00,000 − ₹70,000 = ₹4,30,000

3. Trial Balance

Meaning

Trial Balance is a statement of all ledger balances.

It helps verify whether:

Total Debits = Total Credits

Purpose

  • Check accounting accuracy
  • Detect posting errors
  • Prepare final accounts

Example

Ledger Debit Credit
Cash ₹50,000
Furniture ₹20,000
Capital ₹70,000

Total Debit = ₹70,000

Total Credit = ₹70,000

Viewing Trial Balance

Path:

Gateway of Tally → Trial Balance

Shortcut: T

Real-Life Use

Before finalizing annual accounts, accountants first verify the Trial Balance.

If the Trial Balance is not balanced, errors must be corrected before preparing reports.

6. Ratio Analysis

Meaning

Ratios help evaluate business performance using financial data.

TallyPrime automatically calculates various financial ratios.

Important Ratios

Current Ratio

Measures liquidity.

Formula: Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities

Example: ₹2,00,000 ÷ ₹1,00,000 = 2:1

Meaning: Business can pay short-term obligations comfortably.

Gross Profit Ratio

Measures profitability.

Formula: Gross Profit ÷ Sales × 100

Net Profit Ratio

Measures overall profit efficiency.

Formula: Net Profit ÷ Sales × 100

Debt Equity Ratio

Measures financial risk.

Formula: Debt ÷ Equity

Real-Life Use

  • Investors check ratios before investing.
  • Banks check ratios before granting loans.

Common Mistakes While Analyzing Financial Statements

1. Ignoring Outstanding Expenses

May overstate profits.

2. Wrong Ledger Grouping

Causes incorrect reports.

3. Not Reconciling Bank Accounts

Results in inaccurate cash balances.

4. Incorrect Stock Valuation

Affects profit and Balance Sheet.

5. Viewing Only Profit

Profit alone does not indicate cash availability.

Always analyze:

  • Profit & Loss
  • Balance Sheet
  • Cash Flow

together.

Best Practices in TallyPrime

Maintain Correct Ledger Groups

Example:

  • Salary → Indirect Expenses
  • Furniture → Fixed Assets

Record Transactions Daily

Avoid backlog entries.

Reconcile Bank Accounts Regularly

Ensures accurate financial reports.

Verify Trial Balance Monthly

Helps detect errors early.

Review Financial Statements Monthly

Enables better business decisions.

Backup Data Regularly

Protects accounting records.
