Introduction

A Trial Balance is one of the most important financial reports in accounting. It is a statement that shows the balances of all ledger accounts at a particular date.

The main purpose of a Trial Balance is to verify that:

Total Debit Balances = Total Credit Balances

If both sides are equal, it indicates that accounting entries have been recorded according to the double-entry system.

In TallyPrime, Trial Balance is automatically generated from all voucher entries and ledger postings.

What is Trial Balance?

Definition

A Trial Balance is a summary of all ledger balances showing:

  • Debit balances
  • Credit balances
  • Total debits
  • Total credits

It helps accountants check the mathematical accuracy of accounting records before preparing final accounts.

Why is Trial Balance Important?

Businesses prepare Trial Balance to:

1. Check Accuracy of Accounting Records

Ensures every debit has a corresponding credit.

2. Detect Errors

Helps identify posting mistakes and unbalanced entries.

3. Prepare Financial Statements

Used for creating:

  • Trading Account
  • Profit & Loss Account
  • Balance Sheet

4. Review Business Performance

Provides a quick overview of assets, liabilities, income, and expenses.

How Errors Affect Trial Balance

Error Example

Purchase of ₹10,000 recorded as ₹1,000.

Result

Debit = Credit still matches.

Trial Balance agrees.

But financial statements become incorrect.

Therefore:

Trial Balance checks arithmetic accuracy but cannot detect all accounting errors.

Errors Not Revealed by Trial Balance

1. Complete Omission

Transaction not entered at all.

Example:

Purchase ₹5,000 forgotten.

2. Wrong Account

Rent posted to Salary Account.

3. Compensating Errors

Two errors cancel each other.

4. Principle Errors

Capital expenditure treated as revenue expenditure.

Example:

Machinery purchase posted as Repair Expense.

Interview Questions on Trial Balance

Q1. What is Trial Balance?

A statement showing balances of all ledger accounts to verify debit and credit equality.

Q2. Where can Trial Balance be viewed in TallyPrime?

Gateway of Tally → Reports → Trial Balance.

Q3. Why is Trial Balance prepared?

To check accounting accuracy and prepare financial statements.

Q4. Can Trial Balance detect all errors?

No. It cannot detect errors of omission, principle, or wrong classification.

Q5. Which accounts normally show debit balances?

Assets and Expenses.

Q6. Which accounts normally show credit balances?

Liabilities, Income, and Capital.

Summary

A Trial Balance is the foundation of financial reporting in TallyPrime. It summarizes all ledger balances and confirms that the accounting records follow the double-entry system. Businesses use it daily, monthly, and yearly to verify transactions, identify issues, and prepare financial statements. In TallyPrime, Trial Balance is automatically generated from vouchers, making it one of the most frequently used reports by accountants, auditors, business owners, and students.
